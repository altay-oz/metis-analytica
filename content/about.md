 **<span style="color: #0092BC">metis analytica </span>** is a
*société à taille humaine* — a French expression that goes beyond
simply “small company.” It describes a firm where every client
relationship is direct, every engagement is personal, and no one gets
lost in corporate layers.

Based in Paris, France, we work with corporate R&D and innovation
teams navigating complex technology landscapes — helping them move
forward with clarity, confidence, and the right data at their
fingertips.

## Born out of a real need

TechLand didn’t begin as a product. It began as a limitation.

During his PhD at **Institut Mines-Télécom Business School**, Altay
Ozaygen spent long days, weeks working directly with **PATSTAT**, the
European Patent Office’s large-scale patent database. Extracting
meaningful insights required writing SQL queries, adjusting them,
running them again — over and over.

The real challenge wasn’t just technical. It was methodological.

Understanding a technology landscape is inherently iterative. You
start with an idea, test it, refine it, expand it, narrow it again.
Each step requires balancing recall and precision, exploring different
angles, and questioning your own assumptions. But the tools available
made this process slow, rigid, and difficult to sustain.

What should have been an exploratory process became constrained by the
tools themselves.

TechLand emerged from that gap — not as a predefined product, but as a
solution reflecting the required working approach.

## Founder

Altay began his career after leaving a PhD program in materials
engineering, moving into IT — first as a UNIX/Linux system
administrator, and then progressively toward data and research.

His PhD in management, completed at Institut Mines-Télécom Business
School, added another dimension: understanding how organisations make
decisions, allocate resources, and shape innovation strategies.

This combination — engineering, systems, and management research —
forms the foundation of **<span style="color: #0092BC">metis analytica
</span>**. TechLand reflects the breadth of this intersection.

## What we believe

We believe that understanding technology is not a one-shot analysis,
but a process — iterative, exploratory, and sometimes uncertain.

It is also a fundamentally social process. Technology does not evolve
in isolation: it is shaped by interactions between experts from
different fields, by exchanges between engineers, researchers, and
decision-makers, and by the collective effort to define meaningful
directions and strategies.

Good decisions come from clarity, not volume — from refining the
signal, not accumulating noise.

Our role is to be part of that process: working alongside our clients,
contributing to their thinking, and helping turn complex information
into shared understanding and actionable strategy. This is what
*société à taille humaine* means in practice — being close enough to
collaborate, not just to deliver.
